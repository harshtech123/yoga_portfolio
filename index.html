About Me

Hello, I'm Shruti Gaikwad, a passionate and dedicated yoga teacher with a journey that has transformed not only my own life but the lives of many others. My yoga journey began several years ago when I first discovered the profound impact that mindful movement and breathwork could have on my physical and mental well-being. What started as a personal exploration quickly grew into a deep passion for sharing this ancient practice with others. Over the years, I have immersed myself in various styles of yoga, learning from some of the most respected teachers in the field. My approach to yoga is holistic, combining traditional techniques with modern insights to create a practice that is accessible, enriching, and deeply transformative. As a yoga influencer, I strive to inspire others by sharing not just the physical aspects of yoga but also the mental and spiritual benefits that come with a consistent practice. My classes and content are designed to empower individuals, helping them to build strength, find balance, and cultivate a sense of inner peace, regardless of where they are on their yoga journey.

My Yoga Skills

Hatha Yoga

Hatha Yoga is one of the most traditional forms of yoga, focusing on physical postures (asanas) and breathing techniques (pranayama). It's a gentle practice that emphasizes alignment and slow, deliberate movements. Hatha Yoga is great for beginners and those looking to improve their overall balance and flexibility.

Ashtanga Yoga

Ashtanga Yoga is a rigorous style of yoga that follows a specific sequence of postures, performed in a fast-paced and physically demanding manner. It's designed to build strength, flexibility, and stamina. Ashtanga is suitable for those who are looking for a challenging and disciplined practice.

Vinyasa Yoga

Vinyasa Yoga is characterized by a dynamic flow of postures, where each movement is synchronized with breath. Often referred to as "flow" yoga, it is more fast-paced and energetic compared to Hatha Yoga. Vinyasa classes vary in intensity and can range from gentle to vigorous.

Kundalini Yoga

Kundalini Yoga combines physical postures, breathwork, chanting, and meditation to awaken the energy at the base of the spine and bring it upward through the chakras. It's a spiritual practice that aims to increase awareness and consciousness.
